Suicide Current Issue

On October 14, 2019, there was a message blew the whole internet. Sulli, one of the famous K-Pop celebrities died by suicide. Many were shocked. Many miss her today.


One month and ten days later, Goo Hara, a 28-year-old K-Pop Star, was also found died in her home. Police did not disclose the reason for death. However, we know that Hara has attempted to kill herself in May 2019.


Suicide happens certainly not just among celebrities, it happens ubiquitously in terms of age groups, genders, countries. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), there are about 800,000 people die by suicide every year, which is about one person per 40 seconds.


Also, about every committed suicide, there is an average of 20 attempts.


Suicide is a public issue. We would like to find the relationship between suicide rates with age groups, genders, and countries based on analyzing the suicide data we got from the WHO.

Gender-Continent:



Among all the regions, Europe has the highest suicide rate, while Africa and North America have the lowest suicide rates. Comparing between females and male. Male in all continents has triple to quadruple of the average suicide rate than female.

Conclusion

Overall, we found that male in the age of 35 to 54, who lives in Europe has the largest potential to commit suicide. There are also limitations in our conclusion: our dataset only collected observations from 1985 to 2016, which is not up-to-date; also, for continents like Asia, we were not able to obtain data from three countries, so the observation may be biased.

It is difficult to live as an adult in the world. We have to face many issues, challenges, and downtimes. However, death should not be a way to fix any of these issues. Instead, being alive is the only way we could find solutions out. Fortunately, suicide is preventable.
