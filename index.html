Suicide Current Issue




On October 14, 2019, there was a message blew the whole internet. Sulli, one of the famous K-Pop celebrities committed suicide. Many were shocked. Many miss her today.


One month and ten days later, Goo Hara, a 28-year-old K-Pop Star who is a good friend of Sulli, was also found dead in her home. After several days of investigation, Police decalred her death cause as suicide.


Suicide happens certainly not just among celebrities, it happens ubiquitously in terms of age groups, genders, countries. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), there are about 800,000 people die by suicide every year, which is about one person per 40 seconds.


Also, about every committed suicide, there is an average of 20 attempts.


Suicide is a severe issue that people rarely talk about in the public. Thus, we would like to raise the awareness to the suicide issue by showing the relationship between suicide rates with multiple demographic and economical factors, such as age, sex, and countries based on the WHO suicide dataset.

Key Findings

From the graph above, we see several patterns in this data:

  • The male people live in Russia, aged 35-54 have the highest risk of committing suicide.
  • There is a large amount of people committed suicide at their 35-54.
  • There is a much more male committed suicide than female in 2015.

Let's take a close look

Age-Year

According to the global data, in the year of 1985 to 2016, the suicide number had increased among all age groups. Specifically, a larger number of suicides were committed within the 35-54-year-old group, closely followed by people 55-74-year-old. People 5-14 and 75+ are less likely to commit suicide.


Gender-Continent Relationship Exploration



Among all the regions, Europe has the highest suicide rate, while Africa and North America have the lowest suicide rates. Comparing between females and male. Male in all continents has triple to quadruple of the average suicide rate than female.

Conclusion

Overall, we found that middle aged male in the age of 35 to 54, who lives in Europe has the largest potential to commit suicide.

There are also limitations in our conclusion: The dataset is not complete in the sense that there are more Europe observations across the years and limited observation from other continents. For example, the dataset does not have any observations from China and India. In addition, there are observation gaps between years such as for Cuba, we only have observations from year 1992 to 2015.


our dataset collected observations from 1985 to 2016, which is not up-to-date.



It is difficult to live as an adult in the world. We have to face many issues, challenges, and downtimes. However, death would not be a way to solve any of the life challenges. Instead, seeking for help from your companions could together sort things out. We believe that with the data shown here, we can raise more awareness towards suicidal prevention actions. How to get help

In the US, call the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline at 1-800-273-8255. The International Association for Suicide Prevention and Befrienders Worldwide also provide contact information for crisis centers around the world. (Reference: cnn.com)

If you or your friends are experiencing mental difficulties, you can always reach out to Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) on campus.
